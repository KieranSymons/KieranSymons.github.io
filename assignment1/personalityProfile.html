Meyers-Briggs Test (NERIS Analytics Limited, 2021)

Undertaking the Meyers-Briggs Test found at www.16personalities.com, I was found to be of the “Debater” personality type. According to
this test, debaters enjoy high level problem solving and understanding problems or positions from multiple perspectives. An important part
of the debater’s interaction within a team and their willingness and ability to play the devil’s advocate role in discussions.

What’s your learning style? (PHEAA, 2021)

According to the learning style questionnaire facilitated at www.educationplanner.org, I am a visual learner. The positive traits of a
visual learner are that they are able to visualize tasks and learning material as it is being learned, visual learners learn best from visual
mediums. This could be books, film or hands on experience and tutorials. “Visual learners are attracted to color and spoken language that is
rich in imagery” (PHEAA, 2021).

Honesty Test (Psychologies, 2020)

The online quiz found at www.psychologies.co.uk/tests asks the user to respond to hypothetical scenarios in which you need to choose an
appropriate action. The end result of this quiz was an affirmation of how I feel; that I hate to lie. It does raise the issue of persons like
myself, that although honesty is often the best policy, such a stringent adherence to this belief can make the subtler points of group
diplomacy hard.

What does this mean?

When taking tests, the first response is an interesting one. Occasionally one can feel like the result is completely out of line with your
understanding of yourself, or conversely, it can feel like the test has read into your soul. Being able to look at the result subjectively and
with a grain of salt, can allow improvement in your work and interactions by having a deeper understanding of how best you can act for
success.

Knowing my results will change how I put forth my strengths and weaknesses in the team setting. It is easy to put forward an ill matched role
due to pride or eagerness, however for the best possible outcome for all involved a clear view of the strengths, weaknesses, habits, and
personalities of team members is crucial to development of and interaction within, the team. It is worth mentioning that the views of the rest
of the team are important in this as all the tests preceding this are not considered factual and inescapably accurate. Holding on to one
perspective is no better than having none. It is a known effect of people’s inclination to bend their true response towards one that would lead
to a more favorable result discrediting the validity of these test further.

Cohesion and communication form the basis of good teams, and to make the most of the available resources it can be important to assemble a team
that is well rounded and to be aware of potential personality clashes. When the options are available, choosing persons who are honest, with
integrity and are willing to take responsibility for their work and actions, will create a positive, driven atmosphere.
