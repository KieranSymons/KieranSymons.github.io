My Interest in IT

My interest in IT is very much as an outside observer of the industry and as a consumer if IT related products. I am expecting
that through the completion of this course, I will develop a more nuanced view of the industry, where my place is within it
and be able to further, and expand, my career into the juggernaut that is information technology.

I have always been drawn to tech, from a young age my parents were using computers regularly and their interest followed on to
myself. There was a dramatic up tick in my interest in IT when I began to get involved with video games. One of my most vivid
childhood memories is playing and replaying the demo CD that came out of a Nutri-grain cereal box for the very first Age of
Empires game. From there the next major jump in my involvement occurred when I broke my leg at the age of 16, I had a friend I
would regularly play PC games with as he had two computers, and at that point I spent the better part of 6 weeks gradually
becoming more and more involved in a clan based competitive experience in a well-known first-person shooter, Call of Duty. From
there I began being influenced by new and exciting tech and gadgets, from a laptop-tablet combo to the new Google Assistant Nest
products, I always keep an eye on the new and exciting expansions to the gadget sphere.

I have chosen RMIT as the place to undergo some formal training in the IT industry as I have attended the university previously
in other training. I find the institution to be of a high quality with far reaching contacts and facilities, and their online
course option was the perfect fit for my current situation.
